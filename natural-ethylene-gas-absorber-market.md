<p><p>Natural ethylene gas absorbers are used to extend the shelf life of fruits, vegetables, and flowers by reducing ethylene gas levels in storage environments. Different types of absorbers include Potassium Permanganate (KMnO4), Zeolite, Active Carbon, Pumice, and Others. Potassium Permanganate reacts with ethylene gas and converts it into carbon dioxide and water. Zeolite and Active Carbon absorb ethylene gas molecules. Pumice is a porous volcanic rock that effectively traps ethylene gas. Other absorbers may include combinations of various materials with similar gas absorption properties.</p></p>
