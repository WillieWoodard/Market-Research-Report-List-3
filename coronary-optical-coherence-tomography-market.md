<p><p>Coronary Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT) is a medical imaging technique used to visualize the coronary arteries. Doppler OCT devices utilize the Doppler effect to measure blood flow velocity within the arteries, providing information about blood circulation. Handheld OCT devices are portable and allow for easy maneuverability during imaging procedures. Tabletop OCT devices are larger, stationary systems typically used in medical facilities. Both handheld and tabletop devices produce high-resolution images for accurate diagnosis and treatment planning in coronary artery-related conditions.</p></p>
