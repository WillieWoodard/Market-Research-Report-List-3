There are two main types of indirect ophthalmoscopes: binocular indirect and monocular indirect. </p><p>Binocular indirect ophthalmoscopes are usually worn on the head and provide a stereoscopic view of the eye. They are commonly used in general eye examinations and are more convenient for the examiner.</p><p>Monocular indirect ophthalmoscopes are handheld devices that require the use of additional lenses for optimal magnification. They offer a wider field of view and are often used in more specialized eye examinations or surgeries.</p></p>
