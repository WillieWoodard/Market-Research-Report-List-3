<p><p>Ceramic filtering membranes are categorized into three main types based on pore size and filtration capabilities. Microfiltration membranes have larger pore sizes and are suitable for removing particulate matter, bacteria, and some proteins. Hyperfiltration membranes, also known as ultrafiltration membranes, have smaller pores and are effective in separating macromolecules and proteins. Nanofiltration membranes have the smallest pores and can filter out even smaller molecules and ions. Each type of membrane offers unique capabilities for specific filtration needs.</p></p>
