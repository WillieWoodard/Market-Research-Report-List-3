<p><strong>The Transfer Cases Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Gear-driven</li><li>Chain-driven</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Transfer cases can be divided into two main types based on the mechanism used to transfer power from the transmission to the front and rear axles: gear-driven and chain-driven. Gear-driven transfer cases utilize a set of gears to transmit power, providing durability and high torque capacity. On the other hand, chain-driven transfer cases use a chain to transfer power, offering smoother operation and reduced noise. While gear-driven transfer cases are more common in heavy-duty applications, chain-driven transfer cases are often found in lighter vehicles.</p></p>
