One major trend is the increasing adoption of next-generation sequencing (NGS) technology in PGT-A kits. One type of PGT-A kit is Reversible End Termination Sequencing, which employs a sequencing technique to identify aneuploidies (abnormal chromosome numbers) in embryos. Another type is Iron Semiconductor Sequencing, which uses a semiconductor sequencing technology to assess the chromosomal status of embryos. Both techniques help clinicians select embryos with a higher chance of successful implantation and reduce the risk of miscarriages or genetic disorders.</p></p>
