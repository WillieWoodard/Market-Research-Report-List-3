<p><p>Recombinant DNA (rDNA) technology is a scientific process that involves combining DNA fragments from different sources to create new genetic combinations. In the context of the market, there are two main types: expression system and cloning vector. Expression system refers to the method used to produce and express recombinant proteins, such as using bacteria or yeast cells. Cloning vector, on the other hand, refers to a DNA molecule, often derived from a virus or a plasmid, used to transport and replicate foreign DNA fragments in a host organism.</p></p>
