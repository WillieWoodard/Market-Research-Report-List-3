<p><p>Fire sprinkler pipes are categorized into three main types: seamless steel pipes, welded steel tubes, and CPVC pipes. Seamless steel pipes are made without any joints and are known for their high strength and resistance to corrosion. Welded steel tubes are created by welding steel plates together, providing a cost-effective option with good durability. CPVC pipes are made from chlorinated polyvinyl chloride and are lightweight, easy to install, and possess good chemical resistance. Each type of fire sprinkler pipe offers unique advantages for different applications.</p></p>

<p><p>The fire sprinkler pipes market includes various application segments such as wet pipe systems, dry pipe systems, deluge systems, pre-action systems, foam water sprinkler systems, and water spray systems. Wet pipe systems are the most common and simple type, with water always present in the pipes. Dry pipe systems use compressed air to hold back water until a fire is detected. Deluge systems release large amounts of water simultaneously. Pre-action systems combine elements of both dry and wet pipe systems. Foam water sprinkler systems use foam to suppress fires. Water spray systems use high pressure water to extinguish fires.</p></p>
