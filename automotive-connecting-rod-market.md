<p><p>Automotive connecting rods are typically available in three main types: forged, cast rods, and powder metals. Forged rods are manufactured by shaping heated metal using high pressure, resulting in a strong and durable final product. Cast rods are made by pouring molten metal into a mold, offering a cost-effective solution but with potentially lower strength. Powder metals rods are created by compacting metal powders into a solid form, providing a balance between strength and cost-efficiency in the automotive connecting rod market.</p></p>
