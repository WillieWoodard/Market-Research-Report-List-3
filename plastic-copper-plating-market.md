<p><p>Plating involves using electric current to deposit a layer of copper onto a surface, providing a shiny and protective coating. Electroless plating, on the other hand, does not require the use of electric current. It relies on chemical reactions to deposit copper onto a surface, making it a cost-effective and efficient option for coating various materials. Both plating techniques offer unique benefits and are widely used in industries such as electronics, automotive, and construction for functional and decorative purposes.</p></p>
