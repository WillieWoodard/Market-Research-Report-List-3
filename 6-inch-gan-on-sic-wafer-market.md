Conducting wafers have high carrier concentration and low resistance, making them suitable for high-power electronic devices. On the other hand, semi-insulating wafers have low carrier concentration and high resistance, making them ideal for microwave and RF devices that require minimal parasitic effects. Both types play crucial roles in the semiconductor industry, catering to different applications and requirements.</p></p>
