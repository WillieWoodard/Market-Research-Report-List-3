<p><p>The insulin market consists of three main types: animal insulin, regular human insulin, and insulin analogue. Animal insulin is derived from animals like pigs, and though it was commonly used in the past, it is now less prevalent due to advancements in technology. Regular human insulin is the most widely used type, created using recombinant DNA technology. Insulin analogues are synthetic insulins that are modified to mimic insulin's function and are designed to have specific action profiles, offering individuals more personalized and effective treatment options.</p></p>

<p><p>The insulin market encompasses different types of insulin formulations catering to various time frames of action. Short-acting insulin starts working quickly, usually within 15 minutes and peaks within 1-2 hours. Intermediate-acting insulin has a slower onset but lasts longer, typically around 4-12 hours. Long-acting insulin provides a steady level of insulin for up to 24 hours. Pre-mix insulin combines short- and intermediate-acting insulins in fixed ratios, offering a combination of rapid and extended glucose control. These different insulin options allow individuals with diabetes to customize their treatment plans based on their specific needs.</p></p>
