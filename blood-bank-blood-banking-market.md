<p><p>The blood banking market encompasses various types of blood products that are used for transfusion purposes. Whole blood refers to blood collected from a donor that includes all its components. Red blood cells (RBCs) are the most commonly used blood product, primarily used to treat anemia and other blood disorders. Platelets are small cells involved in blood clotting and are used in patients with thrombocytopenia or those undergoing chemotherapy. Plasma is the liquid portion of blood that contains antibodies and clotting factors, often used for patients with bleeding disorders. White blood cells (WBCs) are used in certain medical treatments and research purposes, including stem cell transplantation.</p></p>
