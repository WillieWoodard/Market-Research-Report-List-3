<p><p>Vascular imaging refers to the process of visualizing blood vessels in the body for diagnostic purposes. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI), Computed Tomography (CT), Ultrasound, Nuclear Imaging, and X-ray are different types of imaging modalities used in this market. MRI uses strong magnets and radio waves to create detailed images, CT scans use a series of X-rays to create cross-sectional images, ultrasound uses sound waves to produce images, nuclear imaging involves the use of radioactive material, and X-ray uses electromagnetic radiation to create images. These modalities help in diagnosing vascular conditions and guiding treatments.</p></p>
