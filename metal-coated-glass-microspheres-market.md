<p><p>Metal coated glass microspheres are categorized into two types based on their internal structure: hollow and solid. Hollow microspheres have empty cavities inside, making them lightweight and ideal for applications requiring low density fillers or insulating properties. Solid microspheres, on the other hand, have a dense structure with no cavities, offering higher strength and durability. Both types of metal coated glass microspheres are widely used in industries such as aerospace, automotive, and construction for functions like lightweight filler materials, reflective coatings, and radar chaff.</p></p>
