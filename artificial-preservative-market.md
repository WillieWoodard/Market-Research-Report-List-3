<p><ul><li>Sorbates</li><li>Benzoates</li><li>Propionates</li><li>Nitrites</li><li>Sulfates</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Artificial preservatives are categorized into different types based on their composition and function. Sorbates, such as sorbic acid, are commonly used to inhibit the growth of mold and yeast. Benzoates, like sodium benzoate, are effective against bacteria and mold. Propionates, such as calcium propionate, are used to prevent mold growth in bread and other baked goods. Nitrites are commonly used in cured meats to prevent bacterial growth. Sulfates, like sodium bisulfite, are used to prevent oxidation in dried fruits and vegetables. Other preservatives include antioxidants and antimicrobial agents.</p></p>
