<p><p>The water supply management market comprises two main types: surface water and ground water. Surface water refers to the water that is found in rivers, lakes, and other above-ground sources. It is collected through various methods like reservoirs, dams, and canals. Groundwater, on the other hand, refers to the water that is below the surface of the earth, stored in aquifers. It is collected using wells and pumps. Both surface water and groundwater are important sources of water supply and require careful management to ensure sustainability and meet the growing demand for clean water.</p></p>
