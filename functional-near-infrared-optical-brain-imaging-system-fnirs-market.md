<p><p>Functional Near Infrared Optical Brain Imaging System (fNIRS) is a type of brain imaging technology that utilizes near-infrared light to measure changes in oxygen levels in the brain. There are different types of fNIRS systems available in the market. FT-NIR Spectroscopy refers to systems that use Fourier transform to analyze the near-infrared light absorption and reflection in the brain. Dispersive NIR Spectroscopy uses a prism or grating to disperse the light into its different wavelengths. Other market types include systems that use Acousto-Optic Tunable Filters (AOTF) or filters to manipulate the near-infrared light for imaging purposes.</p></p>
