<p><p>High content screening stations are specialized laboratory equipment used for automated imaging and analysis of cells and tissues. Two main types of high content screening stations are Brightfield and Fluorescence. Brightfield stations use white light to provide clear images of cells, while Fluorescence stations use fluorescent dyes to visualize specific cellular components. Brightfield stations are widely used for basic cell analysis, while Fluorescence stations are preferred for more advanced applications such as live cell imaging and protein localization studies.</p></p>
