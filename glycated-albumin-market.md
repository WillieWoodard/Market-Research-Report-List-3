<p><p>Glycated albumin is a modified form of albumin, a protein found in the blood. It is formed when glucose binds to albumin, and its levels in the blood reflect average glycemic control over the past two to three weeks. Glycated albumin is considered to be a more reliable indicator of short-term glycemic control compared to glycated hemoglobin (HbA1c), which reflects average blood glucose levels over the past three months.</p><p>The glycated albumin market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. The market growth is primarily driven by the increasing prevalence of diabetes worldwide, as glycated albumin plays a crucial role in diabetes management and monitoring glycemic control. Additionally, the growing awareness about the advantages of using glycated albumin over HbA1c in diabetes management is also contributing to market growth.</p><p>Moreover, technological advancements in diagnostic techniques, such as enzymatic assays and high-performance liquid chromatography (HPLC), have facilitated accurate and efficient measurement of glycated albumin levels, further driving market growth. These advancements have made it possible to conduct routine glycated albumin tests in clinical laboratories, thereby increasing the adoption of glycated albumin testing.</p><p>Furthermore, the rising geriatric population and the increasing prevalence of lifestyle-related diseases, such as obesity and cardiovascular diseases, are anticipated to fuel market growth. These factors act as risk factors for diabetes, thereby increasing the demand for glycated albumin testing.</p><p>In conclusion, the glycated albumin market is projected to witness substantial growth during the forecast period, driven by factors such as the increasing prevalence of diabetes, growing awareness about the advantages of glycated albumin testing, and technological advancements in diagnostic techniques. The market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 8.8% during the forecast period.</p></p>
