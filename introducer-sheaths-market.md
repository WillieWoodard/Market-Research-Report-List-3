<p><p>Introducer sheaths are medical devices used during procedures to guide and place other medical instruments, such as catheters, into blood vessels. Straight sheaths are straight, tube-like devices that allow easy insertion into blood vessels without bending. Curved sheaths, on the other hand, have a pre-formed curve that aids in navigating through complex blood vessel pathways. Both types of sheaths are designed to provide a smooth pathway for the introduction of other medical instruments, ensuring safe and efficient procedures.</p></p>
