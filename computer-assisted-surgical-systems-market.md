<p><p>Computer assisted surgical systems (CASS) refer to advanced technologies that aid surgeons during surgical procedures. These systems can be categorized into different types:</p><p>1. Surgical Navigation Systems: These use real-time imaging and computer systems to guide surgeons during minimally invasive procedures, providing accurate navigation and visualization of anatomical structures.</p><p>2. Surgical Robots: These robotic systems enable surgeons to perform complex surgeries with enhanced precision, control, and dexterity. Surgeons control the robots from a console, and the robots translate their movements into precise surgical actions.</p><p>3. Surgical Planners & Stimulators: These systems assist surgeons in pre-operative planning, simulation, and practice of surgical procedures. They can simulate anatomical structures and patient-specific scenarios, helping surgeons improve their surgical techniques.</p><p>4. Other Market: This category includes various innovative technologies and tools that assist surgeons during procedures, such as augmented reality systems, advanced imaging platforms, and computer-assisted surgical instruments.</p><p>Overall, computer assisted surgical systems enhance surgical accuracy, reduce complications, and improve patient outcomes by combining the expertise of surgeons with advanced technology.</p></p>
