<p><p>Food allergy immunotherapies refer to treatments aimed at reducing or eliminating allergic reactions to specific food allergens. Subcutaneous immunotherapy involves injecting small amounts of the food allergen under the skin, gradually increasing the dosage over time to build tolerance. Sublingual immunotherapy, on the other hand, involves placing drops of the allergen extract under the tongue, also with increasing dosages. Both methods aim to desensitize the immune system to the allergen, allowing individuals to consume the food without experiencing allergic reactions.</p></p>
