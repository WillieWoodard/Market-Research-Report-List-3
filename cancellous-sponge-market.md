<p><p>Cancellous sponge, also known as cancellous bone graft or trabecular metal, is a material widely used in orthopedic and dental surgeries. It is made from human or animal cancellous bone and is utilized in bone grafting procedures for the treatment of fractures, spinal fusion, and tooth extractions. The sponge-like structure of cancellous bone allows for the regeneration and growth of new bone tissue.</p><p>The cancellous sponge market is anticipated to witness significant growth in the coming years. Factors driving the market growth include a rise in orthopedic and dental surgeries, an increase in the geriatric population, and advancements in medical technology. The growing prevalence of musculoskeletal disorders and sports injuries has also contributed to the demand for cancellous sponges.</p><p>Moreover, the market is experiencing a shift towards autologous cancellous sponges, which are derived from the patient's own bone, as they offer better biocompatibility and reduce the risk of disease transmission. This trend is likely to propel the market growth further.</p><p>In addition, the adoption of minimally invasive surgical techniques has increased, leading to a surge in demand for cancellous sponges. These procedures result in shorter recovery times and reduced surgical trauma for patients.</p><p>Furthermore, research and development activities in the field of regenerative medicine and tissue engineering are expected to create lucrative opportunities in the cancellous sponge market.
